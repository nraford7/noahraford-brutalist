Biography

Rajesh Garg

CFO, Transformation Leader & Technology Pioneer

Four Continents · Fortune 500 Executive · Early Tech Adopter

Rajesh Garg is a transformation-oriented CFO and executive leader whose career spans four continents and some of the world's most recognized corporate brands. With deep expertise in finance, strategy, general management, technology, and sustainability, he has consistently delivered outsized value through bold strategic moves and disciplined execution.

His path is marked by pivotal moments where he challenged convention—divesting half a brand portfolio in his first year as CFO, building e-commerce platforms for a fraction of proposed budgets, and creating global shared services infrastructures across 69 countries. He operates with what his teams call "casual intensity"—approachable yet demanding, creative yet rigorous.

The P&G Years: Finance Meets Innovation

Rajesh's career began at Procter & Gamble, where he quickly distinguished himself. In his first year as CFO, he led the divestiture of half the brands in his portfolio—over significant internal resistance—achieving 6x net present value on assets that were no longer strategic for the company.

He then drove strategic planning for Pampers across Latin America, leveraging data analysis to reset the brand's ambition and ultimately wrest market leadership from Kimberly-Clark. This analytical rigor led to an unexpected pivot: writing the original memo for pampers.com during the first dot-com era.

The result was P&G's e-commerce strategy for Pampers—delivered for $5 million versus the proposed $30 million, launched in six languages. It remains Procter & Gamble's largest e-business to this day.

We are overestimating the impact of AI in the next five years, but gravely underestimating the impact in the next ten. In ten years, the world will be a completely different place—humans able to do anything and everything at almost nothing in terms of cost.
— Rajesh Garg, on the AI transformation

Venture & Entrepreneurship

His technology instincts led him to P&G's $100 million Venture Capital Fund, where one investment became an obsession. Rajesh and three colleagues left to build a marketing cloud company, acquiring three businesses along the way. As CFO, he raised $45 million and landed Coca-Cola and Procter & Gamble as customers.

When P&G ultimately acquired the company, Rajesh was the only founder hired back—a testament to his unique ability to bridge corporate discipline with entrepreneurial velocity.

Global Business Services & CFO Roles

At Cadbury Schweppes in London, Rajesh led four global projects to create a complete shared services backbone across 69 countries. This operational transformation positioned him for the CFO role at Cadbury Asia Pacific, overseeing finance, procurement, legal, and IT.

Subsequent roles included stints with Tesco, the British retailer, and a board position at Cipla, India's largest pharmaceutical company, where he mentored Nishant Saxena—who later became CEO of Cipla International.

Landmark Group: A Decade of Consolidation

For the past ten and a half years, Rajesh has served at Landmark Group, leading finance, infrastructure projects, and loyalty programs. His core achievement: transforming what was a set of disparate companies into a single, cohesive entity with robust back-office functions and strong governance.

More recently, he has focused extensively on sustainability—developing deep expertise in how it applies across industries and aspects of life. Though global agendas have slowed, he remains confident the focus will return.
